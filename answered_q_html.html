Calculating your gross yield

The gross yield is the annualised interest rate you are earning from businesses, before fees, bad debt and tax are deducted.

It is a percentage and is calculated based on:

  • Your lending: the interest rates you have secured on your lending to businesses.
  • Reinvestment of repayments: businesses usually make repayments on a monthly basis, so the yield takes into account the cumulative effect when you reinvest these repayments (ie, the effect of compound interest).

x

Calculating your annualised return

Your actual net return will display 30 days after your first loan part purchase.


The actual return is a percentage, calculated to show the annualised return you have earned after fees and bad debt, but before tax, since you started lending at Funding Circle.

Your net return may vary in the short term if you purchase loan parts at a premium or if any of your loan repayments are late. If loan parts are purchased from other investors at a premium you will pay for all of the premium at the time of purchase which will lower your return before it increases over time as you receive interest repayments.

It includes any promotions that you may have earned, but is before tax.

See the full net return calculation, and read more about tax treatment.

x

Calculating your estimated fully diversified return

The estimated fully diversified return shows you the return you would earn if you experienced the estimated annual bad debt rate for each risk band (as shown on the the statistics page).

It is calculated by taking your average bid rate and deducting the 1% annual investor fee and the estimated bad debt for your portfolio. It is an estimated annualised return before tax and assumes you have a fully diversified portfolio lending to over 100 businesses.

Returns are shown before tax, to read more about tax treatment click here.
